    How TVSignals works

    TVSignals charts any stock or crypto and turns classic technical analysis into clear buy/sell signals. It's fast, private, and runs entirely in your browser — no account, no server.

    Where does the data come from?
    • Crypto prices — Binance's public API, live, on every timeframe.
    • Stock prices — Yahoo Finance (routed through a public proxy so the browser can read it).
    • Charts — TradingView's free widget + the lightweight-charts library.
    • Earnings — Finnhub (needs your own free key, added in ⚙ Settings).
    Everything is fetched live and the analysis is computed on your device.
    How does it decide BUY or SELL?
    It runs 8 indicators and scores the result from −100 (strong sell) to +100 (strong buy):
    • Trend — EMA 50 vs EMA 200
    • Trend strength — ADX / DMI
    • Momentum — RSI and Stochastic
    • MACD — bullish / bearish crosses
    • Volatility — Bollinger Bands
    • Volume — OBV confirmation
    Score ≥ your threshold → BUY, ≤ −threshold → SELL, otherwise HOLD. Every signal lists the exact reasons behind it. It then blends all five timeframes (weighting the higher ones more) into one confluence read.
    What do the ▲ / ▼ arrows on the chart mean?
    They mark momentum reversals — a MACD cross, RSI leaving oversold/overbought, a bounce off a Bollinger band, or a Stochastic turn. They alternate buy → sell so each entry has a matching exit, flagging timing points even against the larger trend.
    How are the buy/sell prices in “My position” worked out?
    From the price you enter: the stop-loss is 1.5× the average volatility (ATR) below your entry, snapped just under the nearest support; the sell targets are the next resistance levels above the current price (about 1.5× and 2.5× your risk); the add/buy zone is the nearest support below price. Every level is derived from real volatility and support/resistance — never fixed percentages.
    Is my data private? How is it handled?
    There's no account and no backend. Your watchlist, entry prices, alert rules, and Finnhub key are stored only in your browser (localStorage) and never leave your device. The only outbound requests are to the public price APIs listed above to fetch quotes — nothing about you is uploaded or saved on any server.
    Can I check whether the signals actually work?
    Yes — press Backtest to replay the strategy over historical data and see the win rate, total return, and risk:reward. If it loses on the past, treat that as a warning rather than a green light.
    ⚠ Not financial advice. TVSignals is an educational, rule-based tool. Technical signals lag, whipsaw in choppy markets, and know nothing about news, earnings, or fundamentals. Always do your own research and manage your risk — your trades are your responsibility.
